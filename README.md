# HR-Analytics-Employee-Attrition-Performance
Project Overview

Employee attrition is a major challenge for organizations, as high turnover can lead to increased recruitment costs, training expenses, and productivity losses.

The objective of this project is to analyze employee attrition data and identify the key factors associated with employee turnover. Through exploratory data analysis (EDA), the project examines how overtime, monthly income, department, job satisfaction, work-life balance, and years at the company influence employee retention.

The findings provide actionable insights that can help organizations improve employee satisfaction and reduce attrition rates.

Business Objective

The primary goal of this analysis is to answer the following question:

What factors contribute to employee attrition, and how can organizations improve employee retention?

Business Questions

This project investigates the following questions:

What percentage of employees leave the company?
Does overtime affect employee attrition?
Does monthly income influence employee attrition?
Which departments experience the highest attrition rates?
Does job satisfaction affect employee retention?
Does work-life balance influence employee attrition?
Does tenure (Years at Company) affect employee retention?

Dataset: IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition & Performance

The dataset contains employee demographic information, compensation data, job-related attributes, satisfaction metrics, and attrition status.

Key Findings
Employee attrition remains a significant organizational challenge.
Employees working overtime are substantially more likely to leave the company.
Lower-income employees exhibit higher attrition rates compared to higher-income employees.
Human Resources and Sales departments experience the highest turnover rates.
Lower job satisfaction is associated with increased attrition.
Poor work-life balance is strongly linked to employee turnover.
Employees with fewer years at the company are more likely to leave than long-tenured employees.
Business Recommendations
Reduce excessive overtime and monitor employee workload.
Review compensation strategies, particularly for lower-income employees.
Investigate department-specific challenges within Human Resources and Sales.
Invest in employee engagement and satisfaction initiatives.
Promote healthy work-life balance practices.
Strengthen onboarding and support programs for newer employees.
Continuously monitor attrition metrics and retention performance.
